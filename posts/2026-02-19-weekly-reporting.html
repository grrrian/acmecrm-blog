Weekly Reporting in 10 Minutes: A Dashboard Setup Guide

Feb 19, 2026 · 5 min read

Weekly reports fail when they’re long, late, and full of numbers nobody can act on. A good report is short, repeatable, and tied to decisions.

Rule: If a metric doesn’t change what someone does this week, it doesn’t belong in the weekly report.

The 10-minute setup

  1. Pick 3 outcomes. Example: pipeline created, deals progressed, churn risk reduced.
  2. Choose 5 metrics max. Keep them stable week-to-week so trends matter.
  3. Define a single time window. “Last 7 days” or “This week” (not both).
  4. Add one narrative section. A short paragraph: wins, risks, asks.
  5. Automate delivery. Schedule it and include a link to the dashboard view.

A simple weekly template

  • Wins: 2–3 bullets
  • Risks: 1–2 bullets
  • Asks: what you need from leadership / other teams
  • Metrics: 5 numbers with arrows (up/down) and one sentence each

Demo-friendly data

If you’re demoing on a test environment, you can generate believable data without building a full analytics pipeline:

  • Create ~20 deals with stage transitions spread over the last 14 days.
  • Mark 3 deals as “at risk” with a reason tag (pricing, security review, timing).
  • Show a “weekly digest” view that summarizes stage movement and next steps.
